R: reward for mutual cooperation

T: temptation to defect

S: sucker's payoff

P: punishment for mutual defection


A game is a prisoner's dilemma, if T > R > P > S. It is also usually required that R>(T+P)/2 to guarantee that the payoff pure cooperation is higher than that of alternate cooperation and defection. A popular choice of (R, T, S, P) is (3, 5, 0, 1).



If the game between two players is not just once, e.g. a player is playing the game for many or an unkown number of rounds in the population of multiplayers, we have an iterated prisoner's dilemma. The quesion is, which strategy (to play "C" or "D") allows the player to score most.
